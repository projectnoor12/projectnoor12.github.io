Our Story

At Project Noor, we illuminate what once existed in shadow. We hold the conviction that each voice should be acknowledged—particularly those muted by fear, oppression, or their situation. By using narratives, journals, and video logs, we establish a space where concealed realities surface and previously overlooked experiences receive recognition. Our focus is on authenticity and empathy, employing storytelling as a means to document, heal, and establish connections.

Our goal is to elevate the voices, aspirations, and inner reflections of children from diverse backgrounds—particularly those who are frequently ignored or overlooked. Regardless of whether they originate from shelters, distant villages, city slums, or public schools, we hold the belief that every child has a narrative that deserves to be shared. These kids originate from various backgrounds, encounter distinct challenges, and aspire to unique goals—but the common thread among them is the inner light they all possess. Using narratives, video logs, illustrations, and individual reflections, we establish an environment where their voices can be expressed gently, strongly, and authentically. At Project Noor, we are present to hear, to observe, and to assist their inner light—their noor.

“Noor” translates to “light”—and that is precisely what we strive to provide. Not only visibility, but comprehension. We partner with people and communities whose narratives have been neglected or wiped out, making certain that their voices are protected with respect and honor. By revealing what was previously concealed, we confront prevailing narratives and encourage others to perceive the world through a richer, more accurate perspective.
